October 1, 2006:




This article examines the rise of a significant public discourse on queer history in recent times. It specifically explores LGBT History Month, an events program initiated in February 2005 to honor and acknowledge the accomplishments and lives of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender individuals. Additionally, it reviews the exhibition titled "Queer is Here" at the Museum of London, which debuted in February 2006. The article analyzes the potential exclusions that may arise within these contexts due to the rhetoric surrounding openness and repression.
